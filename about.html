Knowledge as
infrastructure.

I treat content systems the way operations leaders treat any other piece of critical infrastructure — governed, instrumented, and built to scale.

Holly Melone · Tampa Bay, FL

My work focuses on building scalable, governed content ecosystems so customers and internal teams can find the right information at the right moment through search, navigation, and AI-driven retrieval. I specialize in turning fragmented or fast-changing information into authoritative knowledge systems.

By defining governance, information architecture, taxonomy, content lifecycle practices, and style standards, I help organizations create content that scales, stays accurate, and supports real work across teams. The goal is never just more documentation. It’s a system that earns trust over time.

My background spans technical writing, instructional design, UX, training, and technical support — a combination that gives me a uniquely practical understanding of how people search for, interpret, and apply information, and how systems succeed or fail in real-world use.

Strong content systems reduce friction, support automation and AI, and let organizations move faster with confidence.

How I think about it.

A short manifesto

Most knowledge problems are not content problems. They are system problems: missing governance, weak ownership, no agreed taxonomy, no lifecycle, no signal to act on. Adding more articles into a system like that just makes the underlying mess louder.

I’d rather build the smallest functioning system, with clear ownership, a real taxonomy, lightweight governance, and a feedback loop, then grow from there. That’s how content stops being a constant fire and becomes leverage.

AI accelerates this, but only if the underlying system is sound. Structured content, clean metadata, and explicit style standards are what make AI-assisted authoring trustworthy. Otherwise it just produces faster mediocrity.
